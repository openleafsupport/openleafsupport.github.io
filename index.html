Why OpenLeaf exists - Motivations

About OpenLeaf

OpenLeaf initiative exists for readers who want depth in a digital world designed for distraction. It is a calm public reading room for essays, reviews, and ideas, free from algorithmic pressure, advertising noise, and the endless pull of scrolling.

The goal is not only to publish thoughtful writing, but also to create a space where reading itself feels slow, generous, and restorative. OpenLeaf is meant for people who still believe books and reflective writing can sharpen the mind, deepen attention, and offer a healthier alternative to the restless habits of the modern web.

It also stands for the idea that serious reading does not have to feel intimidating or exclusive, and that a quiet, welcoming place can still exist on the internet. Its purpose is simple: to keep reading accessible, thoughtful, and inviting for anyone seeking patience, stillness, and complete thought, while helping build a healthier reading culture that can outlast the noise of the present moment.

About Me

I am a software engineer with close to twenty years of experience as a successful IT professional, and I have been an avid reader since early childhood.

Today we are a family of three: me, my wife, and my daughter. One of my greatest joys is seeing my daughter choose books over screen time and grow into a reader in her own right.

I have always encouraged people, from children to older adults, to read more and step away from social media. That conviction is what led me to start this OpenLeaf initative annonomously: a place to read without distraction, free and ad-free forever, and a small effort to help create a healthier reading culture for the generations growing up in an AI-dominated era.

Why Project Gutenberg mattered

Project Gutenberg showed that free access to books can be generous, durable, and global.

It proved that public-domain literature could be preserved and shared without turning the act of reading into a transaction. That spirit deeply shaped OpenLeaf.

The app is being built as a calm, local-first reading experience inspired by that same ethic: no ads, no subscription pressure, no manipulative loops, just books and the space to read them well.
