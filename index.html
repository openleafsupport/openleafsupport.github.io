Read deeply in an age
designed for distraction.

OpenLeaf is a free, ad-free, local-first reading app built for people who still believe books can sharpen the mind, steady attention, and open up an inner life that endless scrolling never can.

A promise

OpenLeaf will remain free and ad-free forever.

Built for readers who want quiet, uninterrupted access to knowledge — and a way to take back their attention from a distracted world.

Why OpenLeaf exists

We live in a time when attention is constantly interrupted, fragmented, and sold. The modern internet is full of feeds that reward speed, outrage, and repetition. Reading asks for something else. It asks us to slow down, stay with a thought, and let the mind become deeper instead of noisier.

I built OpenLeaf because reading feels more necessary than ever. It is not merely a hobby in the age of doomscrolling; it is a way of recovering patience, inner stillness, and the ability to think in complete thoughts.

During my school and college years, I borrowed books from friends and spent long hours in libraries because buying books was not always possible. That early dependence on shared access never left me. OpenLeaf comes from that memory and that gratitude.

Why Project Gutenberg mattered

Project Gutenberg showed that free access to books can be generous, durable, and global.

It proved that public-domain literature could be preserved and shared without turning the act of reading into a transaction. That spirit deeply shaped OpenLeaf.

The app is being built as a calm, local-first reading experience inspired by that same ethic: no ads, no subscription pressure, no manipulative loops, just books and the space to read them well.
