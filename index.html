OpenLeaf

Read deeply in an age
designed for distraction.

OpenLeaf is a free, ad-free, local-first reading app built for people who still believe books can sharpen the mind, steady attention, and open up an inner life that endless scrolling never can.

Why OpenLeaf exists

We live in a time when attention is constantly interrupted, fragmented, and sold. The modern internet is full of feeds that reward speed, outrage, and repetition. Reading asks for something else. It asks us to slow down, stay with a thought, and let the mind become deeper instead of noisier.

That belief is at the heart of OpenLeaf. I built it because I increasingly felt that reading is not just a hobby anymore; it is a form of mental clarity in a culture of doomscrolling. Where endless scrolling leaves the mind restless, reading makes it more patient, more precise, and more alive.

OpenLeaf is my attempt to create a calm space for reading again — no ads, no subscriptions, no pressure to buy, and no attention traps. Just a peaceful reading experience built around the simple idea that books should remain accessible to anyone who wants to read.

My story

I have always been an immersive reader. Today I proudly keep a personal collection of more than 500 books, but my relationship with reading began long before I could afford to buy any of them.

During my school and college years, money was limited, but the hunger to read was not. I borrowed books from friends, spent long hours in libraries, and read whatever I could get my hands on. Libraries were not just buildings to me; they were doors into wider worlds.

In recent years, I have watched more and more people get pulled into the habit of doomscrolling — consuming more information, but reflecting less. That contrast affected me deeply. Over the last two to three years, I stepped away from social networking sites and turned even more seriously toward reading. The result was simple but powerful: more calm, more depth, and a clearer mind.

OpenLeaf comes from that journey. It is built for anyone who wants books to be accessible without friction — no subscription, no purchase wall, no advertising layer between the reader and the page. Just the quiet freedom to read.

Why Project Gutenberg mattered

OpenLeaf was inspired by Project Gutenberg — one of the most generous and quietly revolutionary ideas on the internet.

Gutenberg proved that literature can be preserved, shared, and made available freely to people everywhere. It turned public domain books into a living library for the digital age. That vision stayed with me.

I wanted to build a reading experience around that same spirit: a local-first, ad-free app where free books remain genuinely free and the reading experience remains calm, convenient, and respectful of the user. No tracking. No manipulative engagement loops. No monetised distraction.

If Project Gutenberg preserved access to books, OpenLeaf is my way of preserving the experience of reading them well.

A promise

OpenLeaf will remain free and ad-free forever.

This app exists for readers who want peace, access, and depth. It exists for students who cannot buy every book they want. It exists for people who miss the stillness of libraries. And it exists for anyone trying to reclaim attention from a digital world that profits by scattering it. If reading can still enlarge the mind, then access to reading should feel simple, generous, and free.
