# Verity - A Dark Story You Can’t Fully Trust

> **Author:** Colleen Hoover  
> **Rating:** 9.5/10  
> **Genre:** Psychological Thriller, Romance  
> **Reading Time:** ~3-4 days  
> **Verdict:** Unsettling, addictive, and quietly disturbing - For me its an unputdownable and **must-read** book 

## Summary

*Verity* follows Lowen Ashleigh, a struggling writer who is offered a rare opportunity to complete a bestselling series for famous author Verity Crawford, who has been left unable to write after an accident.

Lowen moves into the Crawford home to sort through Verity’s notes and manuscripts. What she expects is research material. What she finds instead is something deeply disturbing. Hidden among Verity’s work is an unfinished autobiography filled with chilling confessions about her past, her marriage, and her children.

As Lowen reads further, she is pulled into a version of Verity that feels too dark to be real. At the same time, she grows closer to Jeremy, Verity’s husband, which complicates everything she is discovering.

The story slowly tightens around one central question.  
Is what she’s reading the truth, or something far more dangerous?

## My Thoughts

This is one of those books that hooks you early and refuses to let go.

What worked really well is the structure. The manuscript within the story creates a second narrative that feels invasive and uncomfortable in the best way. It’s hard to look away from it, even when you want to.

The writing is simple but effective. It doesn’t try to be overly clever, which actually helps the tension build naturally. The unease creeps in slowly and stays.

There are soo many good lines that lingered with me. A few not to mention is a crime:

> “Truth is subjective. There’s no such thing as an absolute truth.” - so simple but so true!! 

> “I don’t think you can truly love someone unless you know their darkest secrets.”

> “The things that make you different are the things that make you beautiful.”

I didn't find much imperfections. Its a well-written book, unputdownable. Since I picked it up, I just swallowed it. Only a small thing I felt that the romantic angle was developed a bit too quickly given the psychological weight of everything happening. Other than that, I loved every bit of it.

From a reading experience perspective, this was fast and consuming. I finished it in real quick time despite having a hectic office scehdule at that time. Not because I loved every part, but because I needed to know where it was going.

## What Stayed With Me

A constant sense of unease. The idea that truth can be shaped, edited, and performed. How easily perception can be manipulated. This is not a story that gives you comfort or clarity. It leaves you sitting with doubt.

If you enjoy readers who like dark, morally ambiguous stories that play with perspective, this book will stay with you.

## Final Review

*Verity* is not an easy read, but it is a compelling one. It thrives on discomfort and uncertainty, and while it occasionally stretches believability, it delivers an intense and memorable experience.

It’s the kind of book you finish and then sit quietly for a while, replaying what you just read. I really loved it! 

**Rating: 9.5/10**

- **Genre:** Psychological Thriller, Romance  
- **Recommended For:** Readers who enjoy dark, twist-driven narratives
